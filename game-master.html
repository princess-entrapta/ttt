Game Master

For definition, look [Game Master](Glossary#Game Master)

Considerations

The role as the "Game Master" which carries connotations some of which the rules do not endorse.
While the Game Master does have additional responsibilities over the game unrolling, they do not particularly have a decisive role when it comes to:

  • Conflict resolution. If tensions arise in the table, anyone is welcome to intervene and argue the most reasonable stance.
  • Player invitation (or exclusion) as we believe such decisions to not be taken by a single person, but by a collective, preferably majoritarian decision.
  • Rule bending, as anyone can propose their own rule bend if global consensus is met.
    The game host is, before anything else, a narrator, and a player among other players. Even if they might have a particular importance for the game to set, we are expecting Game Master to know the difference between power and authority.

Core responsibilities

The core responsibility of a Game Master is to lead the way for all players to provide them a game setting they agree or consent playing on, without possible ambiguity, and with minimal discomfort. This involve extended discussions, both public and private, in the following objectives:

  • Managing the game narrative content and tailor it to audience
  • Keeping in check of the enjoyment of Players, including game relationship between everyone, in order to proactively propose adjustments as they are necessary.

Setting

As a game host you are invited to propose a wide setting of your own preference, with agreement and discussion of other players. The overall setting must be shared and known before the game, as to let players decide on how they are going to interact with it.

Homebrewing

Game masters are most welcome to modify or extend the rules by anything of their invention if all players consent on it (see Core Principles). They are invited mention beforehand which rules they intend to extend or modify.

Rules ambiguity

Whenever the rules are being ambiguous, for example regarding granting a situational Cover, or an unpredicted conflict between two rules, the Game Master is invited to give their opinion first on how to solve it. Players silence can be considered consent, and doing so is coherent with the golden rule of the game Core Principles.

Unpredicted narration turns

If the players take a decision that is going to make an unpredicted narration turn (example: someone decides to disband from the party), the Game Master can break from their narration to express themselves out of roleplay, privately or publicly.

In these situations, consulting all concerned Players on the course of action, and transparency regarding the plot taking an unpredicted turn, is encouraged.
