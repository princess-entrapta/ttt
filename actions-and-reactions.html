Actions And Reactions

Players spend their World Interaction phase (sometimes referred as RP phase) interacting in various ways with the game narration.

There are cut in two different kind:

  • Actions that are triggered by players themselves. Note that, to take action, a character does not necessarily need approval from others. They simply do it and roll for success (see World Interaction). Roll failure can have negative consequences, but also generate Stress.
  • Reactions are triggered by the Game Master themselves. They can happen as to determine the outcome of a given event happening. They generally call for a passive feature of characters. There is no stress generated from failing these rolls

Actions and reactions are further divided in Skills, as to provide guidance on what character is proficient or having mastery in what area.

Guidance

A Character can provide guidance on another one roll if they have mastery for it and the other character accepts their help. In that occasion, the roll is considered done With Guidance and gets +1.

Speech

Actions

Deceive

Players can use Active Speech to lie about anything. Their lie comes as being believed on any succesful roll. Any failure will expose the character as lying.

Persuade or Convince

Players can use Active Speech to Persuade or Convice a character to take an action for them. NPCs successfully convinced or persuaded take one action as if they were in the PC party. Under circumstances allowed by Game Master, they can rally next combat.

Distract

Players can use speech to distract other NPCs. On successful distract, the party can take any number of actions that will go unnoticed for a timespan that is considered around 30 real world minutes. They only need to roll Theft if they take an action otherwise forbidden by NPC, it their immediate vincity, such as pickocketing or shoplifting them.

Reactions

Impress, Befriend

Players can be asked to roll speech passively as to provoque a more friendly reaction from a given NPC. The Game Master will determine how the npc will react based on the reaction outcome.

Trading

Actions

Scavenging

Players are allowed to loot after combat. This activity is done through Dice Rolling, and considered a scavenging action.

Scavenging results are not party-shared, unless players actively decide to.

Bargaining

Players can be occasionally offered paid services or items.

The action to bargain can offer discounts, typically 10% on partial success and 20% on full, but can get the character kicked out of the shop on failure.

Reactions

Appraisal

When finding items, or buying any item, players can be asked to roll appraisal to determine if their character can evaluate what the item approximate value.

Failure of appraisal will be left with character think the item is almost worthless, being appraised for 0.1x the value table. Partial success will deduce an approximate value that is 0.5x the item value. Success will deduce the item value.

When an item is appraised, the npc will be able to negociate enough so it can only be sold for at most its appraised value.

Perception

Actions

Searching

Searching for a particular item or clue in an area, can be considered active perception action. On success, the item is found.

Intentions probing

Probing the intentions of a NPC is an active Perception roll. On success, the character is able to determine the true motive of a NPC. On partial success, they only get an instinct of the hostility level of the NPC.

Reaction

Hearing, Seeing, Detail Picking

Such situations can be varied. "You hear sound", or "You see smoke in the distance". Detailed descriptions can be given on a successful roll.

Alertness

Combat roll initiative is determined by perception rolls.

Athletics

Actions

Swimming, climbing, running

When a character needs to perform a phyisical intensive move, it falls into the category of athletics action. Failure of action might result, for climbing, or for swimming in a damage penalty. Players must be warned of possible consequences of their action failing, before they roll.

Athletics experts are encourage to carry a Rope to transform a successful athletics in dragging the party to them. Such actions, unless made particularly difficult by conditions, may not require rolling again.

Forcing something open

Characters can force something ajar (doors, windows or chests), provided they have appropriate tooling or that the object is weak enough. This can require a success on athletics.

Moving large objects

Characters can be required to roll to move large objects.

Seize by force

A Character can seize by force what a npc is holding. Doing so is considered direct agression, but a successful roll could make the NPC disarmed.

Reactions

Fall damage reduction

Successful roll reduce fall damage slightly.

Standing

Successful roll avoid being prone due to various that could push the Character.

Stealth

Action

Theft

A Character can pickpocket or shoplift a NPC under a successful Distract for one item they would possess. Each item stole, if several, needs a separate roll. NPCs cannot be stole items they hold in hands.
A failed theft action will be noticed and cause the NPC to become more hostile.

Unlock

With appropriate tools if necessary, a Character can unlock an object without having the otherwise authorization or key. This is considered a Stealth check.

Disguise, Impersonate

A Character can impersonate a NPC that they would have neutralized. The success of such action is done through a check of this ability.

Assassinate

A Character can silent kill an unsuspecting NPC on a succesful stealth roll. Though the roll can be decided hard roll, the success on it means a silent takedown. Partial success would mean the takedown is successful, but isn't silent. Failure initiate automatic combat with the character turn being passed.

Reaction

Stealth reaction check can be of use if something goes sufficiencly silent or unnoticed.

A sneak attempt past many persons can involve many stealth rolls. Game Master is advised to roll the whole attempt in a single throw, applying appropriate difficulty modifiers when several people are guarding.

Group stealth reaction

When the whole group attempt a sneak, they are considered under Guidance if anyone is master of stealth, but must individually roll. Only a full group success is considered good for group sneak.

Science

Actions

Medicine

Characters don't heal to full automatically after combat.

In any setting that offer a small rest to the players, usually after a combat, they can have the opportunity to heal.

The proposed resolution is as follow.

  • Every Character heal unconditionally for 2 HP.
  • For anybody more injured, anyone else can roll once a medicine skill.
    • Failure means loss of 1 HP.
    • Partial success means recovery of 2 HP.
    • Success means recovery of 4 HP.

The rules allow only one first aid per injured player, and a given helping player can only roll once. Masters in Science can only provide guidance if they are not tasked with a roll on their own.
Medecine can also be actionned to help stabilize dying characters.

Repair

As part of events that can happen to Characters, their equipment can become damaged or break. They can under some circumstances be allowed to repair it, if they carry or get lended the appropriate tools.

Reaction

Lore (Geography, history, religion...)

Science is a passive feature that is asked for by Game Master to roll. Successful rolls will involve knowing of a particular property of an item, of a part of history, or anything remotely similar.
