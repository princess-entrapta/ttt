Core Combat Mechanics

Every unit have a handful of essential stats:

  • Health points (HP) which represent how much a unit can take before dying
  • Defense (Def), which is a score of attack to reach to successfully land the damage
  • Action points (AP), which represent what a unit can do during turn
  • Tenacity point (TP), which represent how much a unit can take before being stunned
  • Movement points (MP), which represent how far a unit can move during turn
  • Special points (SP), which represent how much powerful abilities can be cast

Additionally some are granted by equipment calculation:

  • Range, granted by Weapons
  • Weapon dice (Wd) which represent how deadly a weapon is
  • Base Defense (Def) granted by Armors

All character start typically with 10 HP, 3 AP, 1 MP, 3 SP, 4 TP. Equipment can grant some bonus to these base stats.

Pen and paper considerations

Although there isn't many base stats, a lot of these values like HP, TP, and SP are dynamic during fight. We encourage players to keep track of them with any physical mean they find convenient, such as tokens or dices.

Rounds and turns

Every round, all units take exactly one turn to do any possible action. The turns of units happen in a fixed order starting from the first.

Some actions may take before initiative, such as a surprise attack. They are resolve before combat and may result in enemies or allies being starting already hit.

The first turn, all units on the Players side must roll 3d (see: Dice Rolling), and add their perception bonus. Unlike world interaction rolls, there is no success or failure. This is done to determine the Character priority inside a party. In case of a tie, the Players in tie must roll again, until all ties are broke. The allied NPC, if any, are then added to the priority list, in any order the Game Master decides.

Every round, turn played alternate side to side, starting with the side that have the most units (allies in case of tie), and starting with the unit of highest priority. The Game Master can freely organize the enemy side without any roll.

If there is a side that is overflowing in units compared to the other, the overflowing units all act last.

Every turn a unit dies, the round is reorganized as to still follow these rules. In the example, killing enemy unit 2, will cause all enemies labeled 3 and after, to act one turn sooner. 3 will become the new 2, 4 the new 3 etc.

Playing a turn

Movement

Movement points can be spend to move tiles around. A move typically takes 1 MP, but can take more based on terrain or condition modifiers.

Moving around with this little movement is slow, which is why you can also spend AP as movement. Beware however that doing so, that you sacrifice your turn action!

Movement can happen at any point of the turn. You can move through friendly units if you spend enough movement not to stand through them.

Normal attack rules

Among possible actions, each unit can do a normal attack. This action costs 2 AP.

You then roll attack against unit defense. Be sure to refer Dice Rolling for details on how the attack dice are rolled, and Cover rules for various ways the defense can be modified.

The possible outcome of the attack is determined by comparing the attack and defense scores:

  • The damage is entirely nullified if Defense is at least 2x Attack roll (sometimes referred as Miss)
  • The damage is 1, if Defense is superior (strictly) to Attack roll (Partial hit)
  • The damage is 2 if Defense is inferior or equal to Attack roll (Hit or Full hit)
  • If attack is superior to twice the defense of the target, the target takes 2 damage and loses a tenacity point.

The damage is removed from health. If the targets reach 0 this way, its killed. Although they are taken down for the fight, if they win the fight with causalities, Players can be under certain circumstances be granted Revival.

Line of sight

Ranged attacks, unless otherwise specified will require a LoS between you and your target. If this condition isn't met attacking is impossible, even within range. If LoS is partly, but not totally obtruded (to the Players appreciation) it will grant a cover instead.

Tenacity

A unit that took TP damage can't play more action a turn than they have tenacity points left for it. This includes Movement. It is possible for a unit to play more action and movement point, but only if they didn't take tenacity damage this turn.

At the end of its turn, a unit always regain their maximum tenacity points.

Abilities and SP

Abilities do not only cost Action Points, they also cost Special Points. Characters start combat with their maximum amount of SP, that they can spend on abilities. At the end of their turn, if they did not cast any ability, they regain half their maximum SP, rounded up.

A note on Friendly Fire & Self Damage

Some abilities and effects will grant various area of effect damage. Friendly fire is not possible by the standard rules: all possible explosions that may be provoked by teammates will end up miraculously sparing the same team player, regardless if this is very realistic by the setting. Self damage is however a possible and allowed outcome.

If you intend to play by more realistic, stricter rules on friendly fire, specify this as an addition of your own to the rules (see Game Master).
