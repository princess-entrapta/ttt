World Interaction

The Game Host can setup a variety of contexts that are playable by the rules. Character
must be build according to the setting and chose their proficiency as described in the Character
page.

Characters must chose 1 area of mastery and 1 area of skill, among the following action classes:

  • Speech. This is used to deceive / persuade / distract NPCs.
  • Resource. This is used to scavenge / bargain.
  • Perception. This is used for acuity / guessing intentions / research.
  • Athletics. This is used for running / climbing / pushing.
  • Theft. This is used for hiding / pickpocketing.
  • Science. This is used for first aid / general knowledge.

Resolution

Any action attempted by a Character that calls for any of their base skill must be rolled with 3d, as described in the Dice Rolling.

They get +1 for skill and +2 for mastery.

3 or below: Failure.
4 or 5: Partial success.
6 and above: Success.

Partial success will mean the Character can succeed, but that will cost them considerable energy to do so. They can chose to fail and suffer consequences, they can be offered half what success would offer if it applies, but in other situations where outcomes is binary, they can chose to succeed but receive a stress marker.

Players with stress markers receive a -1 penalty to all their non-combat rolls. They can only receive up to 2 stress markers, at which point any further partial success can no longer be converted into success.

Players can relieve stress by one of the following way:

  • Following your character's arch and roleplay their passive negative trait. This trait may have gameplay effects such as affecting a further roll or one of your teammate's roll. If applicable that case, the roll gets a -2 penalty. This removes 1 stress marker.
  • Having an uninterrupted night of sleep. The Game Host may or may not grant this at the session end. This removes all stress markers.

Typical world interaction

Medicine

Players don't heal to full automatically after combat. This activity is done through Dice Rolling of their medicine action (Knowledge).

The proposed resolution is as follow.

  • Character can heal for a small, fixed amount, of 1 to 2 HP.
  • For anybody more injured, anyone else can roll once a medicine skill.
    • Failure means loss of 1 HP.
    • Partial success means recovery of 2 HP.
    • Success means recovery of 4 HP.

The rules allow only one first aid per injured player, and a given helping player can only roll once.

Scavenging

Players are allowed to loot after combat. This activity is also a Dice Rolling of their scavenging (Resource) action.

All characters can roll, and get money and gear proportionally scaled to their success scale.

Scavenging results are not necessarily party-shared, especially not if someone have a greed trait.

Shopping

Players can be occasionally offered the opportunity to shop for improvements. Such improvements costs must be tailored so a Character can sometimes offer themselves an improvement, almost always a consumable, but not much more.

The action to bargain (Resource) can offer discounts, typically 10% on partial success and 20% on full, but can get the character kicked out of the shop on failure.

Athletics

Other actions are generally plot driven. Generally, they are social driven, but interaction with environment can also happen with the athletics action: need to cross a river? Make a high jump? Climb a wall? Escape chasing NPCs?

When such actions fail, they might result in health penalty. Other times, they can be deadly. In these situations, the Game Guests must be warned of consequences of failure before rolling.

A trick to avoid killing outright a Character is to separate them from the group, and make it disappear, out of sight. Then introduce savior NPC that would have been there to save them with their special abilities. Such saving may come at a cost however; at minimum, Stress costs.

Perception

Perception is a passive feature. Unlike other actions, Game Host decides when it is to be rolled. Failure involve generally no immediate punishment, however Game Guests will be a lot more prepared to what is to come if they perceive it first !

Such situations can be varied. "You hear footsteps" "You see smoke in the distance" or, in social situations "You have a bad feeling about this NPC" etc.

Perception is a small reward business feature, compared to other active actions, but Game Host is encouraged to ask frequently to use it.

Sometimes, Perception can also be considered active, as in search; finding a needle in a haystack is active perception check. Failing the search should result in a stalemate here again.

Speech

Speech is straightforward. It involves straight NPC manipulation of something that's against their will. Failure will turn them hostile, possibly starting combat.

Theft

Theft moves include hiding, moving silently, pickpocketing, and other actions that can involve subtlety to hide what you're currently doing to anyone nearby.

Failures of theft can result in hostile reaction, or simply in covers being blown up and a combat to start.

Science

As perception, Science is a passive feature that is asked for by Game Host to roll. Successful rolls will involve knowing of a particular property of an item, of a part of history, or anything remotely similar.

When used so, it is no risk low reward.

Science can also be used actively as to craft or to repair. Failures will result in destruction of components without any success.
